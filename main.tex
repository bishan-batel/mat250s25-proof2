Let \Rn n,\Rn m be Euclidean spaces.

Let $T:\Rn m\to\Rn n$ be a linear transformation with corresponding $n\timesm$ matrix $A$.

Let  $\vec x_1, \vec x_2, \dots, \vec x_k \in \Rn m$ be vectors such that the set of vectors
$\set{T(\vec x_1), T(\vec x_2), \dots, T(\vec x_k)}$ is linearly independent.

\noindent \rule{\textwidth}{1pt}
\linebreak

Prove that the set of vectors $\set{\vec x_1, \vec x_2, \dots, \vec x_k}$ is linearly independent.
